# KEV Analysis — Daily Analyst Report: 2026-04-19

## TL;DR

Three-day KEV dry spell continues — no catalog updates since 2026-04-16 — but the broader exploit landscape moved meaningfully today. **Two AI-tooling products produced the two most exploitation-ready CVEs of the cycle: CVE-2026-39987 (Marimo) was weaponized within 9h41m of disclosure into a 662-event NKAbuse botnet campaign via Hugging Face, and CVE-2026-21858 (n8n "Ni8mare", CVSS 10.0, ~100k exposed instances) has a public PoC and unauth RCE via HTTP Content-Type confusion.** Neither is in KEV yet, but both are textbook HTTP-parsing-adjacent candidates and go on the watch list. Meanwhile, the CVE firehose itself softened: NVD published only +19 CVEs in the 24h to 2026-04-19, dropping the April extrapolation to ~5,793 — week-16 is now the slowest full week of the post-Glasswing era, which weakens the "AI is flooding the vuln pipeline" framing at today's cut.

### The two headline non-KEV disclosures of the last 24–48 hours

**CVE-2026-39987 — Marimo pre-auth RCE (CVSS 9.3, disclosed ~2026-04-10, exploited 2026-04-11).** The Marimo Python-notebook web UI exposes a terminal WebSocket endpoint (`/terminal/ws`) with no authentication. Unauthenticated attackers get a full PTY shell. Sysdig's threat-research telemetry recorded **662 exploit events between April 11 and April 14 from 11 source IPs across 10 countries**, deploying an NKAbuse variant that uses the NKN P2P protocol for C2 — distributed via **Hugging Face model artifacts** as the initial infection vector. The window from public disclosure to first observed exploit was **9h41m**. This is the strongest disclosure-to-exploit compression data point of the cycle and the cleanest "AI-tooling product becomes its own attack vector" case so far: Marimo is adopted heavily by ML practitioners, and the payload delivery channel (Hugging Face) targets that same audience. Watch-list candidate (server-side). Not yet in KEV — but if CISA is going to add a non-Microsoft library this cycle, this is the one.

**CVE-2026-21858 — n8n "Ni8mare" unauth RCE (CVSS 10.0, disclosed 2026-04-17/18, public PoC).** Horizon3.ai and Cyera published detailed writeups of a Content-Type confusion flaw in n8n's webhook/file-handling pipeline. Sending crafted HTTP requests with manipulated content-types lets an attacker override internal state, read auth secrets, forge admin sessions, and achieve arbitrary code execution. All versions ≤1.65.0 vulnerable; fixed in 1.121.0 (Nov 2025). **~100,000 internet-exposed n8n instances per Cyera.** No in-the-wild reports yet — but the exposure surface and PoC maturity make this the closest thing to a guaranteed future KEV entry on today's table. Server-side watch-list add.

Together these two bugs are instructive: both are AI-adjacent automation tooling (Marimo for notebooks, n8n for LLM workflows), both rely on HTTP-facing surfaces that were never threat-modeled like a real web framework, and both are downstream of the AI-stack growth curve rather than of AI-driven discovery. The "AI ecosystem as vulnerability supply chain" thesis is getting more evidence than the "AI as discoverer" thesis this week.

## Mythos Signal Check

**Assessment unchanged from yesterday: weak but real signal in three Glasswing-target products (FreeBSD 1, wolfSSL 1 press-confirmed, Bouncy Castle adjacent), no in-the-wild exploitation evidence yet.** No new Mythos-attributed CVEs today. Two non-Mythos data points were relevant for the framing:

**The Marimo/Hugging Face exploitation story is specifically *not* a Mythos signal.** Attackers exploited a human-discovered RCE using conventional scanners and dropped a conventional RAT via a conventional model-repo poisoning vector. The only connection to AI is that the victim pool (ML engineers) and the delivery channel (Hugging Face) are both downstream of AI adoption. If anything, this weakens the "AI accelerates discovery" reading and strengthens the "AI creates new attack surface" reading. Both can be true simultaneously.

**The ActiveMQ KEV entry (CVE-2026-34197) continues to age without consensus narrative.** Per Horizon3.ai's disclosure, this bug was traced by Naveen Sunkavally using Claude as an analysis tool in ~10 minutes. That is "Claude-as-research-multiplier" in a non-Anthropic, non-Glasswing workflow — a credit pattern that is quietly becoming more common than direct Anthropic attribution. If the July disclosure counts only Anthropic-internal attribution, the "40 attributed CVEs" framing will be an undercount of the actual Claude-in-the-loop exploitation population. That matters for how we frame "is AI changing the landscape?": the answer may already be yes, just distributed across many third-party researchers rather than concentrated at Anthropic.

**Cumulative signal across the run (no change):** 3 Claude-in-the-credit-line disclosures (CVE-2026-4747 FreeBSD NFS autonomous; CVE-2026-5194 wolfSSL press-confirmed Mythos-assisted; CVE-2026-5588 Bouncy Castle Carlini+Claude). Zero of the three are in KEV. The *exploitation* half of the thesis remains untested.

**Devil's advocate, steady.** If we are wrong about the Mythos signal entirely, the wrong will show up as: July 2026 disclosure reveals a CVE pool dominated by Carlini-led human-driven research with Claude as a productivity tool (not autonomous discovery), at severity no higher than industry baseline, in products already well-audited. Today does not resolve this in either direction.

## Category & Trend Analysis

**CVE firehose is softening.** NVD published only +19 CVEs in the 24h window to 2026-04-19 — a sharp drop from the ~203/day pace of April days 1–18. Daily rate now ~193/day. April extrapolation moved **down** from 6,082 → 5,793 since yesterday. Week-16 (Apr 13–19, the first full post-Patch-Tuesday week) came in at **1,164 published / 166 per day — the slowest of the last three weeks and ~28% below W15's 231/day**. The most likely explanation is Patch-Tuesday batching: W15 gorged on April 14 disclosures, and W16 is the trough on the other side. That still matters for interpretation: **if Glasswing were already inflating publication volume, the first full week after the launch should not be the slowest.** It is. That is disconfirming evidence, at least at the 12-day horizon.

**Counter-reading.** NVD publish latency is 4–8 weeks, and the April 7 Glasswing launch means Anthropic-discovered CVEs cannot yet appear in volume. Furthermore, coordinated vendor-patch timing could produce a lumpy future signal rather than a smooth daily rate. I don't dismiss either — but by end of May both excuses lose force. I'll start weighting "Glasswing-volume signal is absent" more heavily after 2026-05-15.

**Stack-layer distribution of today's *non-KEV* disclosures (24h window):** library/framework-heavy — SAIL ×2, Marimo, n8n, ChurchCRM, NovumOS. Five of six are HTTP-facing in some form (Marimo WebSocket endpoint, n8n webhook HTTP endpoint, ChurchCRM web app, SAIL indirectly via image hosting, NovumOS is the local-priv exception). The HTTP-parsing thesis continues to dominate the top-of-funnel for critical disclosures.

**CWE families, 24h window:** input validation / Content-Type confusion (n8n), missing authentication (Marimo, the most embarrassing single-sentence CWE of the cycle), file upload / archive extraction (ChurchCRM), memory corruption (SAIL ×2), privilege escalation (NovumOS). Injection/validation remains the dominant critical-severity family — no visible uptick in memory-corruption share that would suggest AI-fuzzing dominance. Consistent with our running observation that Mythos, if it is landing, is not producing the classic fuzzer-style distribution.

**New product families vs. 2021–2024 baseline:** Marimo and n8n are genuinely novel entrants — AI-stack automation products that didn't exist or weren't deployed at scale pre-2024. If either lands in KEV, that will be the first "AI-tooling-product-itself-is-exploited" KEV entry. Tracking.

## Thesis Challenge (Non-HTTP Library Exploits)

**No new counter-examples today.** Searched across April 2026 KEV additions and the 24h disclosure window; no library/framework CVE has reached KEV via a purely non-HTTP vector this cycle. The closest near-misses:

- **SAIL library CVEs (40492 / 40494)** are memory-corruption in image decoders — they *could* be exploited via local file processing (non-HTTP), but in practice images reach users via HTTP delivery. Not a counter-example yet.
- **CVE-2025-60710 (Windows link following)** was added to KEV April 13 and involves link-following, not HTTP — but Windows itself is excluded (OS, not library).
- **CVE-2026-5588 (Bouncy Castle)** is a crypto library bug found by Carlini+Claude; not yet in KEV, and the demonstrated exploitation paths are primarily TLS-adjacent (i.e., HTTP-parsing-adjacent) rather than non-HTTP.

The absence of counter-examples is itself evidence for the thesis, but partially reflects detection bias: library exploitation via non-HTTP channels (e.g., file-parsing libraries hit via email attachments, crypto libraries hit via stored credentials) is harder to observe and attribute. No table updates this run.

## Big Picture

**What today tells us that yesterday didn't, in one paragraph.** The most exploitation-active CVEs of this week are not the Mythos-attributed Glasswing-target library CVEs — they are two AI-tooling products (Marimo, n8n) whose own HTTP-facing surfaces were never properly threat-modeled. The Marimo case set a 9h41m disclosure-to-exploit record and deployed a functioning P2P botnet through Hugging Face. That is a more immediately consequential AI-ecosystem security story than anything in the Glasswing disclosure trickle, and it suggests that the biggest near-term security externality of AI adoption may be "every ML engineer runs three new HTTP services on their laptop without hardening" rather than "AI discovers novel bug classes at scale." Both stories can be true, and both probably are, but the downstream-attack-surface story is the one producing body counts this week.

**Is Glasswing/Mythos changing the vulnerability landscape yet?** Still no at the aggregate level. April is extrapolating to ~5,793 — 8% *below* March and well below every projection scenario. KEV at 14/month pacing with 2026 baseline. No Glasswing-target products in April KEV. W16 (the first full post-Glasswing-launch week) is the slowest of the three weeks measured. The honest read: **12 days in, zero aggregate signal**. The NVD-latency argument buys Anthropic until mid-May before absence becomes evidence.

**Is AI adoption changing the landscape yet?** Yes — but through a different mechanism than we were initially watching for. The CVE-2026-39987 / CVE-2026-21858 pair today, CVE-2026-40352 (FastGPT NoSQLi) yesterday, and the Marimo-via-Hugging-Face delivery vector together describe a picture where AI-tooling *products* are producing a steady stream of critical, exploitable HTTP-facing bugs at pace. That is a separate thesis from "AI discovers bugs" and it's arguably more urgent operationally — a CISO whose data-science team runs Marimo or n8n internally has a bigger immediate problem than one waiting for the next Firefox Mythos advisory.

**If I were a CISO this week:**
1. **New this week:** asset-inventory your Marimo and n8n deployments *today*. If either is internet-exposed and pre-patch, you have a 99%+ likelihood of being probed within 24 hours. Treat as P0.
2. Carry forward: SharePoint (32201), FortiClient EMS (x2), Ivanti EPMM, Acrobat (34621), wolfSSL SBOM audit, Thymeleaf audit.
3. De-prioritize relative to last week: BlueHammer (33825). Still unpatched-in-KEV 12 days post-POC; if it was going to hit KEV on PoC alone, it would have.

**What to watch, next 7 days:**
1. Does CVE-2026-39987 (Marimo) enter KEV? Given documented 662-event exploitation campaign, same-cycle KEV add is the most likely single data point. If CISA does *not* add it within 7 days, that is material evidence the "active exploitation ⇒ KEV" rule is more conservative than reputation suggests.
2. Does CVE-2026-21858 (n8n Ni8mare) attract observed-exploitation reports? 100k exposed + public PoC + CVSS 10.0 = expect first ransomware-related hits within 2–3 weeks.
3. Thymeleaf 40477/40478 — still the cleanest "library-CVSS-9-with-PoC becomes KEV-listed" test we have open.
4. First post-April-7 Firefox or wolfSSL CVE with explicit Anthropic/Mythos attribution. Mid-to-late April is the earliest plausible NVD appearance window given publish latency.
5. Any CISA KEV addition for a library-layer CVE via a non-HTTP vector — that would populate the Thesis Challenge table for the first time.
