The "free coverage" read. Triaging C+H+M is operationally impossible. The Crit/High filter we already run catches 87% of medium exploitation when applied to the same packages — the structure test generalizes down with the same miss profile (DoS, CSRF, open-redirect, info-disc, chain primitives) it shows at C/H. Mediums in NP packages with injection CWEs are getting caught by the model whether we treat them as the target population or not.
The other side. 134 of 135 records are in NP-classified packages — on this dataset NP+DI essentially collapses to "does the bug have an injection CWE." The discriminator is doing weaker work than at C/H, where NP-vs-non-NP is roughly 55/45. Hacker S+A (26%) is the actual filter at this severity tier, and 0 of 4 KEV-confirmed CVEs cleared S+A. The "free" part is partly free because mediums-in-NP-packages-with-injection-CWEs is the population the model is built around.

The hypothesis (mostly) holds. Mediums take meaningfully longer to be exploited than recent C/H — sub-7 median 136d vs C/H 11–34d in 2023–2024. The long right tail is striking: p75 of 316d, max of 2,893d (nearly 8 years from publish to first exploit). Researchers periodically revisit old medium CVEs to publish working exploits long after disclosure.
The complication. The 3 KEV-confirmed sub-7 entries have a 25-day median — comparable to the 11–34d C/H KEV baseline. So when an attacker actually decides to exploit a medium, they don't take much longer than they do for criticals. The "longer TTE" effect is mostly in the ExploitDB tail, not in real attacker activity. Honest read: nothing protects you from a medium that an attacker decides to weaponize; what's longer is the average wait, dragged out by exploits researchers wrote years after the fact.
